What's In a Name?

The name Andes is pulled from Antisuyu which is Quechua word meaning East (Anti) and Quadrant (Suyu) originally referred only to the eastern region of Inkan civilization in the Andes. The Inkans themselves referred to the Mountains as Tawantinsuyu or Realm of the Four Parts.

Pronunciation of Tawantinsuyu

The names for each region were:

  • Chinchaysuyu (Northern Region)
  • Antisuyu (Eastern Region)
  • Contisuyu (Western Region)
  • Qullasuyu (Southern Region)

An example of how one of these regions would be pronunded in Quechua. Example is for Qullasuyu.

A final note is that while we're learning about the Mountains through the Inkans, Quechuas and Aymaras, there were many other groups that have own words for land they inhabit like the Mapuche, Nasa, and Kawesqar.

The Wiphala

Each Suyu had it's own Wiphala to denote itself. The flag is made of a 7x7 patchwork with seven colors established diagonally. Since the time of the Inkans, The wiphalas have become symbols symbols of Pan-Andean pride and resistance across all the countries that include them. When traveling through each region, the wiphala will show in the bottom left corner!

Disclaimer

As stated elsewhere, the information here about the Andes is pulled from Inkan civilization as its the most documented and largest sprawling culture of the Mountains. It's worth noting that alongside not emcompassing the every group of people's experience living in the Andes, the history we do have was documented by invading and violent Europeans who filtered the history and perspective through their lens to fit their agenda.

We still use this information even with this caveat because due to cultural differences in how history is viewed, these documents are the most fleshed out ones we have though taking into account current Indigenous Andean perspectives is equally important.
